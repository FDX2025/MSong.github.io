Focus Areas

Research

Photonic System Design

System-level thinking, modelling, and design strategies for advanced photonic and electronic systems.

Focus on system-level thinking, modelling, and design strategies for advanced photonic and electronic systems. This includes passive intermodulation phenomena, signal integrity in high-frequency connectors, and integrated photonic circuit design methodologies.

Photonics System Design Modelling

Machine Learning

Machine learning methods for modelling, optimisation, and intelligent system analysis.

Explore machine learning methods for modelling, optimisation, and intelligent system analysis. Research focuses on applying data-driven approaches to complex physical systems, including surrogate modelling, neural network-based optimisation, and deep learning frameworks for engineering design.

Deep Learning Optimisation Data-Driven

Advanced Algorithms for Optimal Design

Computational methods and algorithmic frameworks for precise, efficient, and scalable design optimisation.

Develop computational methods and algorithmic frameworks for precise, efficient, and scalable design optimisation. This includes evolutionary algorithms, gradient-based methods, global optimisation strategies, and hybrid approaches for multi-objective engineering problems.

Scholarly Output

Publications

Journal SCI 2022

High-precision Mathematical Modelling of Passive Intermodulation in Connectors

M. Song, Q. Jin

Electronics Letters, 2022

A high-precision mathematical framework for modelling passive intermodulation (PIM) phenomena in connector assemblies. The model achieves improved accuracy over existing approaches by incorporating higher-order nonlinear contributions and a systematic parameter fitting methodology.

Journal Accepted

A Novel Passive Intermodulation Model of Coaxial Cable Assemblies with Distributed and Point-source Nonlinearities

R. Liu, Q. Jin, M. Song, et al.

Journal — publication details to be confirmed

A novel PIM model for coaxial cable assemblies, distinguishing between distributed and point-source nonlinearities. The approach provides improved physical insight and prediction accuracy for complex cable assembly systems under realistic operating conditions.

Patent

A Fitting Method for a Passive Intermodulation (PIM) Mathematical Model

Patent No. 023108822605

A fitting method covering passive intermodulation mathematical models, providing a systematic approach to parameter estimation and model calibration suitable for engineering applications in high-frequency connector and cable assembly systems.
